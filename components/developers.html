Basic concepts

NOMO allows you to manage all your payouts to sellers, contractors and service providers through a single powerful API. It is PSP agnostic and supports line item (shopping cart) data, variable commissions, FX, KYC as well as wallet to wallet (P2P) transactions. Here are just some of the benefits our partners find particularly intriguing about NOMO:

  1. You can hold funds in escrow as long as you like and initiate partial payouts
  2. It’s easy to update, reverse, delete and split payments between multiple accounts
  3. We work with your favourite PSP - or recommend one if you’re not sure
  4. Our team is dedicated to help you integrating NOMO with your platform

Setup

Getting you up and running is pretty straightforward. We create an account with a master user for you and provide you with Sandbox access. Then you can create as many admin users as you need for your business and start onboarding your customers. You do that by creating Accounts. Accounts can have multiple Users (e.g. your customers are businesses themselves) and multiple Wallets (e.g. one for EUR and another one for GBP). KYC data is also associated with each Account. Don’t worry, we provide a fully-fledged identity verification solution for you!

Funding

We will hold all funds for you in escrow in our segregated and safeguarded trust account. There are three options how transactions can be funded:

1. PSP

Your existing PSP transfers money into our trust account. NOMO is PSP agnostic. So whether you’re using Adyen, Braintree, Stripe or someone else all you need to do is change your business bank account with them to our trust account where funds will be held segregated and safeguarded in accordance with UK/EU regulation.

2. Peer-To-Peer

Some of your sellers might just as well be buyers on your platform. You can let them use funds in their wallets to pay for purchases. Your benefit is that no PSP fees apply. We offer an optional QR code based authorisation solution to tighten security on those wallet to wallet transactions.

3. Prefunding

If you want to speed up payouts (i.e. not wait for funds sent by your PSP/acquirer to settle) or even try NOMO without implementing any changes with your existing PSP you can simply pre-fund our trust account. If you choose to integrate with your exiting PSP, we’re required to run a quick background check on them. We will promptly provide you with trust account information. In case you need help to find the best PSP for your business, let us know. We might be able to offer you a great package deal with DOCOMO Digital Payment Services.

Payments and wallets

When a purchase happens on your platform you create a Payment through our API. Each payment can consist of multiple Lines in case you want to treat different items of your shopping basket independently from each other (e.g. the buyer purchased goods from different sellers). Each line is then associated with multiple Transactions. Typically, you would split up the purchase amount into two Transactions, namely the seller’s revenue (Merchandise revenue) and your platform commission (Net revenue). VAT is another example you can account for with a separate Transaction.

A Transaction will typically credit a Wallet. If you credit a Wallet and the actual funds haven’t been received in the trust account yet (i.e. your PSP hasn’t paid them out yet), NOMO will keep the Transaction in a ‘pending’ state. Once enough funds are in the trust account the status of that Transaction automatically changes to ‘completed’ thus creating a credit in the corresponding Wallet.

If you allow your sellers to trade in different currencies you can simply provide them with one wallet for each currency. You can also transfer money between different currency wallets. NOMO will provide you with midmarket FX rates prior to each transaction. Another purpose for multiple wallets can be to manage loyalty points.

You can also manage Refunds and Disputes via the NOMO API. As long as funds haven’t left the trust account they can immediately been clawed back.

Finally, NOMO is monitoring Transactions to support all relevant rules around Anti Money Laundering (AML) and Anti Terrorist Financing (ATF).
